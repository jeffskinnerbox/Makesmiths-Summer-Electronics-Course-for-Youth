# Bill of Materials (BOM)
Costs, quantities, and purchase links are intentionally excluded here.
Key items will be purchased via ProTechTrader Pack 1 purchase and other items supplied via Makersmiths.

Assumes 3 teams with 2 students per team

## Hardware Per Team (IN Pack 1)
Each team has one at least one [Make: Electronics Component Pack 1][06]
(the [ProTechTrader][07] pack mirroring Experiments 1–11). Pack 1 supplies, across the course:
- **Test leads** with alligator clips; **9-volt battery connectors**.
- **Resistors** (assorted values incl. 47Ω, 470Ω, 1K, 2.2K, 4.7K, 10K, 100K, 220K, 470K, 1M) and a **3-amp fuse**.
- **Generic LEDs** and **low-current LEDs**.
- **Potentiometer** (1K) and **trimmer potentiometers** (incl. 500K).
- **Toggle switches** (SPDT/DPDT) and **tactile (push) switches**.
- **DPDT 9-volt relays**.
- **Capacitors** — ceramic (small µF values) and electrolytic (incl. 1,000µF).
- **2N2222 transistors** (note: *not* P2N2222).
- **Solderless breadboard** (single-bus, 700 tie-point type) and **22-gauge solid hookup wire** in multiple colors.
- **8-ohm loudspeaker** (1″–2″).
- Small hand tools: **screwdrivers, long-nose pliers, wire cutters, wire strippers**.
- Digital **multimeter** (with measurement leads) and **batteries** (9-volt and AA).

## Supplemental Materials for the KidWind Capstone (NOT in Pack 1)
Experiments 25, 26, and 28 require items beyond Pack 1 — to be procured separately
- A **large screwdriver** and a **paper clip** (Experiment 25 electromagnet).
- **Neodymium magnets** — a small cylindrical magnet (≈3/16″ × 1.5″) and a larger one (≈3/4″ × 1″), axially magnetized (Experiment 26).
- **Magnet wire** (≈26-gauge, several hundred feet) and/or extra **22-gauge hookup wire** (~200 ft) for winding generator coils.
- A **1N4001 switching diode**, an extra **1,000µF capacitor**, a **47-ohm resistor**, and extra **low-current LEDs**.
- Materials for the spinning-generator rig (whole class builds): **wooden dowel** (~1/2″), **PVC pipe** (~3/4″ ID),
  and two pieces of **1/4″ plywood** (with a 1″ hole saw / Forstner bit available).

## Consumables
- Experiment 5 lemon battery: **lemons** (or a squeeze-bottle of lemon juice), **copper-plated coins** (e.g., US pennies),
  and **galvanized (zinc-plated) steel brackets**.

## Shared Tools Provided by the Makerspace
- Per-team multimeter is included in each kit; the makerspace additionally provides **safety glasses**, shared hand tools,
  and a **drill with a 1″ hole saw / Forstner bit** for the capstone generator rig.

## Software & Student Computer Requirements
- **None.** This is an analog electronics primer — no computers, no programming, and no Arduino (those belong to the later courses).
  Students need no laptop or internet for class.

## Each Student Brings / Has
- Their own copy of **[*Make: Electronics*, 2nd Edition][01]** (printed, or the free [PDF][02]).
- A **build journal** (notebook) and a pencil; a simple calculator is handy for Ohm's Law work.
