# Electronics for Makers
The course will be based on some proven materials,
the book "[Make: Electronics, 2nd Edition][01]", by Charles Platt.
The book can be purchased for $25 or its free for a [PDF version][02].
There are official kits, supporting the experimentation documented in the book.
[ProTechTrader][03] is the only vendor tested & approved by by the author.

The [kits are split into three packs][04], mirroring the book's experiments:
* **Kit Pack 1:** Experiments 1–11 in Chapters 1 & 2, Amazon for $100 to $160 (the Deluxe option includes a digital multimeter + batteries; the course buys the Standard pack and supplies meters/batteries separately — see `BOM.md`)
* **Kit Pack 2:** Experiments 12–24 in Chapters 3 & 4, Amazon for $130 to $150 (this includes soldering iron)
* **Kit Pack 3:** Experiments 25–34 in Chapters 5 & 6, Amazon for $110

## Beginner Electronics for Makers Course
Using Pack 1 only, schedule 6 session, 2 hours once a week:
* Experiments 1 to 5 - Voltage, resistance, Ohm's Law, multimeter basics
* Experiments 6 to 8 - Switches, relays, oscillators
* Experiments 9 to 11 - Capacitors, transistors, light & sound
* ADDER FOR KIDWIND - Experiments 25, 26, 28 - Magnetism, Tabletop Power Generation, Making a Coil React

## Intermediate Electronics for Makers Course (POTENTIAL FUTURE COURSE)
Using Pack 2 only, schedule 6 session, 2 to 3 hours once a week:
* Experiments 12 to 15 - Soldering, LEDs, intro to ICs, alarm circuit
* Experiments 16 to 20 - 555 timer, logic gates, pulse/tone generation
* Experiments 21 to 24 - Combinational logic → electronic dice simulator capstone project build

## Advanced Electronics for Makers Course (POTENTIAL FUTURE COURSE)
Using Pack 3 only, schedule 6 session, 2 to 3 hours once a week:
* Experiments 25–34 - Electromagnetism, Filtering Frequencies, Radio, Arduino

Things to consider:
* I think this plan might be aggressive for some kids, but this gives older/more experienced kids the opportunity to move faster.
* We could have the kids work in teams and share a kit,
  This keeps cost down, but if they have there own kit ($100 to $160), they have the opportunity to experiment at home.
