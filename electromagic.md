# Ground Loop
A ground loop is an unwanted circular path in an electrical circuit that occurs when two or more devices are connected to a common ground but have different ground reference potentials. This voltage difference forces unintended electrical current to flow through the ground wiring, manifesting as a loud hum or buzzing in audio and video
