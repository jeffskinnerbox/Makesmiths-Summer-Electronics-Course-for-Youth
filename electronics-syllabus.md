# Electronics Primer Course

**Based on [*Make: Electronics (2nd Ed.)*][01] by Charles Platt**<br>
**6 Weeks · 1 Class/Week**

The purpose of the course is to give the students some intuition
about how electricity works and how it can be made useful.
Students will work in teams and can share kits.
If a student would like to work on experiments at home,
they can purchase their own kit from Amazon for $100 to $160.
(Kit [Pack 1][02] mirrors Experiments 1–11 in Chapters 1 & 2 of the book and includes a digital multi-meter and batteries.)

---

## Week 1 — Electricity is a Flow
**Experiments 1–2** in *Make: Electronics (2nd Ed.)*

Taste electricity safely (9V battery on tongue), learn what voltage and current mean.
Analogy: electricity is water flowing through a pipe. Introduce the multimeter — measure voltage on batteries and household items.

---

## Week 2 — Resistance and Ohm's Law
**Experiments 3–5** in *Make: Electronics (2nd Ed.)*

Use resistors to control current. Build simple circuits on a breadboard.
Measure resistance; confirm Ohm's Law (V = IR) with hands-on readings. Analogy: a resistor is a narrow spot in the pipe.

---

## Week 3 — Switches, Relays, and Automatic Control
**Experiments 6–8** in *Make: Electronics (2nd Ed.)*

Wire toggle and push button switches. Build a relay circuit — let a small current control a big one.
Explore a simple oscillator that blinks an LED. Analogy: a relay is a drawbridge you raise with a small handle to let big trucks cross.

---

## Week 4 — Capacitors, Transistors, Light & Sound
**Experiments 9–11** in *Make: Electronics (2nd Ed.)*

See how a capacitor stores and releases charge (the water-tank analogy). Use a transistor as a switch and amplifier.
Build a circuit that makes light and sound react to signals.

---

## Week 5 — Magnetism and Coils
**Experiments 25–26** in *Make: Electronics (2nd Ed.)*

Explore permanent magnets and electromagnetic fields. Wind a coil and watch it respond to a changing magnetic field.
Analogy: a coil is like a spinning waterwheel — movement makes electricity.

---

## Week 6 — Generating Your Own Power
**Experiment 28 + Wrap-Up** in *Make: Electronics (2nd Ed.)*

Build a tabletop generator; feel the resistance when a load is connected.
Recap all six weeks — from Ohm's Law to generating electricity.
Students demo one concept of their choice to the class.

---

## Materials (Per Student)

- 9V batteries
- Multimeter
- Breadboard kit
- Resistor, capacitor, and LED assortment
- Small relay
- Transistor
- Magnet set
- Enameled wire for coiling

> **Safety note:** All experiments run at 9V or less.
